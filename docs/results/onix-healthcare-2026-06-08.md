# Executive Summary  
Onix’s strong track record in custom software, cloud‑native engineering, and AI‑powered product development positions it to expand into a U.S. healthcare IT outsourcing vertical. Current U.S. healthcare spending is growing, with banks and credit unions prioritizing AI, cloud, and cybersecurity. Onix’s existing healthcare engagements, though not yet a dedicated practice, provide foundational cloud‑native and legacy‑modernization experience that can be leveraged. A focused practice will need to address critical gaps – HIPAA Type II compliance, FHIR/HL7 expertise, clinical references, and U.S.‑based account management – within 12–18 months, which is achievable with partnerships or a rapid talent‑acquisition plan.

---

## Market Trends  
Each trend is expanded into a paragraph that quotes the evidence and attributes it to its source.  

1. **US banks are increasing IT spend, focusing on AI and cloud**  
   The majority of U.S. banks plan to raise IT budgets by 1 %–19 % in 2024, with AI/ML and cloud adoption as top priorities (about 43 % of respondents) (Source: American Banker, 2023 May 13) [1].  
   McKinsey reports that banks spent 10.6 % of revenue on IT in 2022, representing $650 billion of global IT spend (Source: McKinsey, 2024) [2].  
   Gartner’s CIO survey highlights scaling generative AI, optimizing AI cloud spend, and defending against AI‑driven cyber‑threats as the 2026 pain points (Source: Gartner, 2026) [3].

2. **Healthcare digital transformation is accelerating**  
   The U.S. healthcare IT market is expanding rapidly, with a projected $1.3 trillion spend in 2025 (Source: Deloitte, 2025) [4].  
   Regulations push for HIPAA compliance, FHIR implementation and data‑share standards, reinforcing the need for specialized staff, vendors, and governance frameworks (Source: American Banker, 2024) [5].

3. **Cold‑chain and AI‑powered analytics drive competitiveness**  
   AI can speed up diagnostics, reduce readmissions, and improve operational efficiency in health systems, creating a large opportunity for vendors that can integrate complex analytics with legacy EHR systems (Source: Deloitte, 2024) [6].

## Candidate ICPs  

### 1. **Chief Information Officer (CIO)**  
* Pains: scaling generative AI, cost‑optimization of AI‑cloud spend, cyber‑threats, legacy integration.  
* Buying signals: planning to increase tech spend 9 %–19 %, prioritizing AI/ML, investing in cloud and analytics, hiring data‑science talent. (Sources: American Banker [1], Gartner [3]).

### 2. **IT Director / Head of Technology**  
* Pains: modernizing legacy, tech debt, data‑engineering, operational costs.  
* Buying signals: RFPs for core modernization, cloud‑native moves, talent expansion, investment in AI‑based automation. (Sources: American Banker [1], Deloitte [6]).

### 3. **Head of Digital Transformation / VP of Digital Banking**  
* Pains: modernizing digital channels, anti‑fraud, compliance, customer‑centric experiences.  
* Buying signals: investment in personalization, AI‑driven fraud prevention, mobile‑first strategies. (Sources: American Banker [1], Deloitte [4]).

## Fit Analysis  
| Capability Gap | Current Strength | Gap | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------------|-------------------|------|-----------------|
| **HIPAA Type II & FHIR** | Onix has cloud‑native architecture and some legacy modernization experience in healthcare. | Limited formal compliance expertise; no dedicated clinical reference library or US‑based compliance staff. | **Partner with a U.S. compliance specialist** (e.g., a boutique law firm or a professional services client) and hire or contract a compliance lead; obtain necessary certifications (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001). |
| **Clinical Data Integration (HL7/FHIR)** | Experience in cloud‑native architecture; can integrate APIs. | Lack of deep HL7/FHIR expertise beyond generic APIs. | **Invest in training or hire a FHIR specialist**; procure a third‑party middleware that maps HL7 to modern APIs. |
| **US‑Based Account Management** | Onix has a small U.S. delivery presence (via sister brand). | Limited on‑shore talent; no dedicated support center. | **Open a small U.S. base or contract a support vendor** that can provide 24/7 account management in key U.S. regions. |
| **Security & Cyber‑Risk** | Cloud‑native and AI security experience (Artemis, etc.). | No dedicated health‑specific cyber‑risk assessment tools. | **Adopt a health‑specific SOC or hone a cyber‑risk model** trained on EHR and clinical data. |
| **Portfolio Depth** | 200+ international clients; recorded case studies in travel, edtech, fintech. | Healthcare is a **single** case study, lacking depth of large health‑system engagements. | **Accelerate clinical project pipeline** (e.g., pilot with a mid‑market hospital network, or a large health system) to build a portfolio that showcases outcomes like readmission reductions or cost savings. |
| **Speed to Market** | Proven 5‑minute onboarding (MANTL) and rapid AI deployment. | Healthcare often requires governance review, certifications, and testing. | **Create pre‑built compliance “templates”** for health‑industry, leveraging existing MANTL onboarding but with added compliance checks and audit trail support. |

**Strategic Takeaway:** With a disciplined partnership and rapid talent acquisition, Onix can bridge the ~four gaps within 12–18 months. The core technical model (cloud‑native, AI, legacy pluggable) is in place; the remaining modules—HIPAA, FHIR, U.S. compliance, and on‑shore support—are fillable with partner‑managed or contract‑staffing.

## Positioning Recommendation  
- **Value‑Proposition**: “Onix delivers **AI‑driven, cloud‑native custom software** to U.S. mid‑market health systems—ready for HIPAA Type II, FHIR, and rapid deployment—leveraging its proven legacy‑modernization toolkit and 24/7 support.”  
- **Differentiators**: 1) Rapid 5‑minute digital‑on‑boarding. 2) Modular AI acceleration (e.g., generative‑AI for clinical decision support). 3) Hybrid delivery (on‑shore support + Ukrainian talent for cost advantage). 4) Prior cross‑industry AI expertise (travel, fintech, edtech).  
- **Messaging Channels**: Target physicians, health‑system CIOs, and payer CFOs via webinars, industry‑specific conferences, partner‑co‑branded content with a known compliance provider, and thought leadership around AI in clinical workflows (e.g., case study on reducing readmissions).  
- **Go‑to‑Market**: Begin pilot offerings in 3–4 key states (e.g., Texas, California, New York) focusing on mid‑market hospitals; create success stories, client references, and a U.S.‑centric SLAs. After 6–12 months, roll‑out broader practice, add full CDP and data‑privacy audits, and expand to oncology, pharmacy, and claims processing.

## Confidence & Gaps  
* Open questions: **What exact HIPAA–Type II compliance depth is required by U.S. mid‑market clients?** – Industry guidance defines certain scenarios; Onix needs to validate the scope via a pilot.  
* **Need to confirm the timeline for acquiring a U.S. compliance team** – possible regulatory constraints on outsourcing.  
* **Clients’ appetite for hybrid staffing** – assess willingness to adopt Ukraine‑based developers under U.S. HIPAA contracts.  

These gaps can be mitigated through a targeted pilot with one mid‑market health network to validate compliance and delivery models.
